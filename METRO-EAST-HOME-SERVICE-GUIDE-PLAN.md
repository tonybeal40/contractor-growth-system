# Metro East Home Service Guide Plan

## The Model

This business should **not** be built on fake listings, fake locations, or duplicate business profiles.

The safe, scalable model is:

1. `All-Pro Construction & Landscape` remains the real contractor brand.
2. `Metro East Home Service Guide` becomes the homeowner demand and routing brand.
3. One CRM catches all inbound leads.
4. All-Pro gets first shot at the best-fit jobs.
5. Overflow, out-of-scope, or delayed-response jobs can later route to vetted partners.

## The Moat

Pages are copyable.

The moat is:

- lead-source data
- city + service demand data
- budget and timing data
- response-speed data
- project outcome data
- routed lead history
- review and proof aggregation

That is what turns a contractor site into a local project network.

## Brand Roles

### Brand 1: All-Pro

Purpose:
- book jobs directly for All-Pro
- rank for core contractor and service-intent searches
- prove trust with photos, reviews, project pages, and direct quote paths

Core examples:
- `bathroom-remodel-belleville-il.html`
- `deck-builder-belleville-il.html`
- `pressure-washing-belleville-il.html`
- `yard-cleanup-ofallon-il.html`

### Brand 2: Metro East Home Service Guide

Purpose:
- capture homeowner demand earlier in the search
- rank for comparisons, costs, planning, and best-next-step content
- intake jobs that are not ready for a contractor-specific quote yet
- eventually route jobs to All-Pro first, then partners

Core examples:
- `best-deck-repair-ofallon-il`
- `bathroom-remodel-cost-belleville-il`
- `landscaping-cost-edwardsville-il`
- `find-a-contractor-collinsville-il`

## What We Do Not Do

- No fake Google Business Profiles
- No fake office locations
- No fake reviews
- No duplicate business names stuffed with cities
- No doorway spam pages with no added user value
- No pretending a lead-gen page is a separate local contractor

Already in place:

- local page generator
- review hub
- direct quote flow

- `metro-east-contractor-match.html` intake layer

These are the raw materials for the lead network.

## Immediate Build Sequence

### Phase 1: Tighten All-Pro direct capture

- keep building high-intent city/service pages
- keep homepage links strong
- keep sitemap and schema clean
- improve quote flow and lead tagging

### Phase 2: Build the Guide layer

- launch public pages that are clearly help / compare / planning pages
- examples:
  - cost guides
  - compare guides
  - best-in-city guides
  - “how to hire” guides
  - project match desk

### Phase 3: Build routing logic

Every lead should carry:

- source
- service
- city
- budget
- timeline
- quote intent
- owner availability
- booked / lost / referred outcome

### Phase 4: Partner network

Once enough demand exists:

- All-Pro gets first right of refusal
- route overflow and non-fit jobs to vetted partners
- track referrals cleanly
- disclose clearly when a request may be matched or routed

## Data Model

Each lead should eventually support:

- `source`
- `landing_page`
- `city`
- `service_needed`
- `budget_range`
- `timeline`
- `quote_intent`
- `photos_ready`
- `assigned_to`
- `status`
- `booked`
- `lost_reason`
- `routed_to`
- `referral_fee_status`

## Public Disclosure Rule

If a page is:

- an All-Pro contractor page, it should say All-Pro
- a help or match page, it should say it is a project intake or match page
- a routed lead page later, it must disclose that clearly

Never blur those lines.
