# Wild Bill Lead Ops

Last updated: July 1, 2026

## Mission

Wild Bill exists to create revenue movement for All-Pro and Bill's List Metro East.

It should not only check that forms work. A healthy run must produce one of these:

- a stronger homeowner lead path
- a proof or SEO improvement
- a Bill's List promotion asset
- a partner prospect list
- a contractor recruitment script
- a follow-up action for a real lead

## Lead Lanes

### 1. Homeowner Leads

Goal: get estimate requests for All-Pro and Bill's List.

Sources:

- All-Pro service pages
- Bill's List project-match page
- Nextdoor posts
- Facebook posts
- Google Business Profile
- organic Google/Bing searches
- referrals and reviews

Output:

- form submission
- phone call
- text/callback
- quote request

### 2. Contractor Partner Leads

Goal: recruit trustworthy local contractors for future Bill's List Pro Network participation.

Best early categories:

- electrician
- plumber
- HVAC
- roofer
- tree service
- junk removal
- cleaning / make-ready
- flooring / drywall / paint
- concrete specialist
- fence specialist

Output:

- company name
- category
- city
- public phone
- website
- contact URL or public email
- source URL
- fit score
- outreach status

### 3. Contractor Growth Leads

Goal: find local businesses that may need lead generation, website work, SEO, or a better quote system.

Fit signals:

- no website
- broken or old website
- no mobile-friendly quote path
- outdated photos
- weak city/service pages
- no review links
- no tracking or conversion form
- active business with poor online presentation
- public hiring/sales language
- public ads with weak landing pages

Output:

- company name
- category
- city
- public phone
- website
- public contact path
- source URL
- one specific reason they may need help
- recommended offer: website refresh, SEO pages, form tracking, reviews workflow, or Bill's List partner lane

## Public Sources Wild Bill Can Use

Use public business information only:

- company websites
- Google Business Profiles viewed manually or through allowed tools
- chamber of commerce member directories
- BBB public business pages
- city/local contractor registration pages when public
- trade association directories
- public Facebook business pages
- public LinkedIn company pages
- public Nextdoor business pages
- public job posts
- public contact pages

Do not use:

- dark-web data
- leaked data
- private residential contact lists
- bought consumer lists
- scraped personal phone numbers
- automated mass texting
- fake identities
- hidden or deceptive lead forms

## Prospecting Workflow

1. Pick one target city and one trade.
   - Example: `Belleville electrician`
   - Example: `O'Fallon plumber`

2. Pull 5 to 10 public business records.
   - Use the company site as the preferred source.
   - If no company site exists, record the public profile source.

3. Record only business-facing contact details.
   - Company name
   - Public phone
   - Website
   - Contact page or public business email
   - Source URL

4. Score the fit.
   - 5 = clear fit and likely needs leads/website help
   - 4 = good local company with weak conversion path
   - 3 = possible partner but needs review
   - 2 = too competitive with All-Pro or weak fit
   - 1 = skip

5. Choose the next action.
   - partner recruit call
   - website/lead-system pitch
   - keep for later
   - do not contact

6. Human approval before outreach.
   - Wild Bill can prepare lists and scripts.
   - Tony/Bill should approve before sending messages or making calls.

7. Log the result.
   - called
   - left voicemail
   - emailed/contact form
   - interested
   - not interested
   - do not contact

## Daily Wild Bill Run

1. Check live forms and lead routing.
2. Check if any new lead needs follow-up.
3. Ship one homeowner-demand move.
4. Pull or enrich 5 partner prospects.
5. Draft one call script or social post.
6. Record what changed.

If there are no lead-flow issues and no prospecting output, the run is weak.

## Weekly Wild Bill Run

1. Publish or prepare 3 Nextdoor/Facebook posts.
2. Add or improve one proof section on the site.
3. Review top service/city pages.
4. Add 20 to 40 public partner prospects.
5. Pick 5 highest-fit businesses for human-approved calls.
6. Update Bill's List pages or partner pitch copy based on what the market says.

## Call Script: Partner Contractor

> Hey, this is Tony with Bill's List Metro East and All-Pro. We're building a local project-match lane for Metro East homeowners, starting around Belleville and O'Fallon. I found your company while looking for real local [category] pros. Are you taking new [category] jobs right now if the project is a good fit?

If yes:

> Great. The goal is not random shared leads. We want city, project type, timing, budget range, photos when available, and a clear homeowner contact path. Would you be open to being reviewed for the first partner shortlist?

## Call Script: Website / Lead Help

> Hey, this is Tony. I work on local lead systems for contractors in Metro East. I found your business while checking [city] [category] searches. You look like a real local company, but I noticed [specific issue: no quote form / no city pages / old website / no project photos]. Are you looking for more calls or estimate requests this month?

If yes:

> I can show you the exact page or form path I would fix first. No pressure. The first step is usually getting the right service page, quote form, tracking, and follow-up in place.

## What Wild Bill Must Never Do

- Do not collect private personal data.
- Do not send bulk spam.
- Do not text businesses or homeowners automatically without consent.
- Do not claim Bill's List is neutral if paid participation affects routing.
- Do not create fake partner listings.
- Do not invent reviews, jobs, licenses, or service areas.
- Do not call a lead "qualified" unless the source and reason are recorded.
