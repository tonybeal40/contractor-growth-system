Invoicing, Payments & Card Processing
MUST ASK
▼
Must Ask
How do you currently invoice customers — paper, email, text, software?
Manual invoicing = massive time leak and late payments. This is often a $10K+ fix.
Must Ask
Do you accept credit/debit cards? If yes, what processor (Square, Stripe, etc.)? What are you paying in fees?
Most small contractors overpay 0.5–1% on processing fees. Easy upgrade opportunity.
Must Ask
How long does it take to get paid after a job? What % of invoices are 30+ days late?
Collections lag kills cash flow. Auto-pay and recurring billing fixes this.
High
Are any customers on recurring auto-pay or monthly billing? Would you want that?
Recurring billing is the #1 revenue stability upgrade for lawn companies.
Standard
Do you use any field service or job management software (Jobber, ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro)?

Customer Outreach, CRM & Follow-Up
MUST ASK
▼
Must Ask
Where do new customers come from right now? (referrals, Google, Nextdoor, yard signs, etc.)
Whatever's working gets 10x'd. Whatever's missing gets built.
Must Ask
When someone calls or texts for a quote — what happens? Walk me through the process start to finish.
The quote-to-close gap is where most small businesses lose 30–50% of leads.
Must Ask
Do you follow up with leads who don't book? How many times? By call, text, or email?
Most leads need 3–5 touches. If the answer is "once," there's immediate revenue on the table.
High
Do you reach back out to past customers at the start of each season? How?
Reactivation campaigns typically recover 20–35% of lapsed customers.
High
Do you have a customer list with phone numbers and emails? How many contacts?
This is the most valuable asset in the business. If it doesn't exist, building it is priority #1.
