Who should apply

Small, capable trade companies with real proof.

Owner-led and smaller residential companies are especially welcome, including solo operators and teams of roughly 1–15 people. Larger qualified companies may also submit when the project and service area fit.

  • Kitchen, bathroom, shower, carpentry, drywall, flooring, painting, and finish specialists.
  • Deck, porch, fence, patio, concrete, retaining-wall, and outdoor-living crews.
  • Landscaping, mowing, cleanup, grading, drainage, tree-service, and hauling companies.
  • Roofing, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, handyman, cleaning, make-ready, and specialty trades.

Minimum review items

  • Real business or legitimate sole-proprietor identity and working contact information.
  • Current certificate of insurance appropriate to the work, plus applicable licensing or registration.
  • W-9 and other tax or compliance documents provided later through an approved secure process.
  • Tools, equipment, experience, references, project photos, and service-area proof.
  • Ability to review and sign a written project scope, pricing, schedule, and responsibility agreement.

How the relationship works

Every project stands on its own written agreement.

Calling someone a subcontractor does not decide their legal classification. The actual work relationship, independence, project scope, and applicable Illinois and federal law control.

1. Capability review

All-Pro reviews your trade, business history, service area, proof, insurance status, tools, references, and current capacity. Submission alone creates no relationship.

2. Project-specific fit

If a possible project fits, the parties review the homeowner scope, access, timing, permits, safety needs, documentation, pricing, and who is responsible for each task.

3. Written terms first

Work should begin only after project review and signed terms. Compensation, payment timing, change orders, documentation, and responsibilities belong in a written agreement.
