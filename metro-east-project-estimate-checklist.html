What affects price

Why some projects need a quick look before a real number.

Real homes have slopes, drainage, old materials, access problems, hidden damage, and code or permit questions. A quick site review keeps the written estimate tied to the actual conditions.

Decks, patios, and concrete

Price changes with size, framing condition, old tear-out, stairs, railings, drainage, grading, concrete thickness, and access for material.

  • Send photos of the whole area.
  • Measure rough width and length if you can.
  • Note soft boards, cracks, slope, or standing water.

Bathrooms and kitchens

Remodel pricing depends on fixture moves, water damage, tile, cabinets, flooring, electrical, plumbing, and whether the layout stays the same.

  • Send photos of walls, floor, and fixtures.
  • Tell us what must stay and what can change.
  • Share if there are leaks or soft spots.

Landscaping and small jobs

Cleanup, drainage, mulch, rock, fencing, pressure washing, and handyman jobs depend on scope, hauling, access, and how quickly you need it done.

  • Send driveway/access photos.
  • Tell us if debris needs hauled away.
  • Mention if you are selling or under a deadline.
Estimate prep

Before you call Bill, gather these details.

  1. Your city and neighborhood or nearest cross street.
  2. The service you need: deck, fence, remodel, landscaping, concrete, patio, cleanup, repair, or small job.
  3. Photos from the street, from the work area, and close up on damage or problem spots.
  4. Your goal: repair what is there, replace old material, improve curb appeal, get ready to sell, or build something new.
  5. Your preferred timeline and any hard deadlines.
  6. Any access limits, gates, pets, HOA notes, drainage concerns, or previous contractor issues.
