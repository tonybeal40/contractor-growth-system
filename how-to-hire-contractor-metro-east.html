Step 1: Always Get a Written Estimate

A verbal quote is worth nothing. Before any work starts — before any deposit — you need a written estimate that includes:

  • Exact scope of work (what is included, what is not)
  • Materials specified by brand or grade (not just "composite decking")
  • Estimated start date and completion timeline
  • Total price and payment schedule
  • How change orders are handled if scope changes

🚩 Red Flags to Watch For

These warning signs should make you pause before hiring any contractor in Metro East.

Asking for Cash Only or Large Deposit Upfront

A fair deposit is 10–30%. Anyone asking for 50%+ before work starts — or demanding cash only — is a red flag. Always have a paper trail.

No Written Contract

If they will not put it in writing, they will not be accountable for it. A handshake deal protects neither of you — but it hurts the homeowner more.

No Proof of Insurance

Always ask for a certificate of liability insurance and workers' comp. If a worker is injured on your property and the contractor has no coverage, you could be liable.

Pressure to Sign Immediately

"This price is only good today." Legitimate contractors do not pressure homeowners. If you feel rushed, walk away.

Suggesting You Skip the Permit

"We can skip the permit and save you money." This exposes YOU to fines, failed home inspections at resale, and work that cannot be verified as safe.

Hard to Reach After Deposit

Calls go unanswered. Start date keeps getting pushed. Ask about communication expectations upfront — before the deposit.

7 Questions to Ask Every Contractor

Ask these before you sign anything. A reputable contractor will answer all of them without hesitation.

1
Can you provide current insurance and applicable license documentation?
Ask to see the certificate. It should list liability insurance and workers' comp.
2
Will you pull the permits?
The contractor should handle permits — not you. If they say no or suggest skipping, find someone else.
3
Who will be on the job site daily?
Some contractors win the job and send subcontractors you have never met. Know who is actually doing the work.
4
Can you provide 2–3 local references?
Recent references in Metro East specifically. Call them. Ask if the crew showed up on time and cleaned up daily.
5
What does cleanup look like daily?
A professional crew cleans the job site at the end of each day. Ask specifically — do not assume.
6
What is the payment schedule?
A fair schedule: deposit at signing, progress payment midway, final payment only after you are satisfied with the walkthrough.
7
Is there a final walkthrough?
A professional contractor walks through the finished project with you before collecting final payment. No exceptions.

Common Questions

How do I check if a contractor is insured in Illinois?

Ask for a Certificate of Insurance (COI) directly from the contractor. It should list liability insurance ($1M minimum is standard) and workers' compensation. You can call the insurance company to verify it is active.

What is a fair deposit for a contractor in Metro East?

10–30% of the total project cost is standard. Anyone asking for more than 50% upfront should raise questions. Never pay 100% upfront under any circumstances.

The contractor's bid is way lower than others. Is that good?

Not always. Get 3 estimates and understand the middle range. A bid 40% lower than the others often means cutting corners on materials, planning upsells via change orders, or they are desperate for cash flow.

Do I need permits for a deck or fence in Belleville or O'Fallon?

Yes for most projects. Decks over 30 inches from grade require permits in most Metro East municipalities. Skipping a permit to save a few hundred dollars can cost thousands at resale. Permit and inspection responsibilities should be confirmed in the written scope for the project and jurisdiction.

The contractor disappeared after my deposit. What do I do?

File a complaint with the Illinois Attorney General's Consumer Protection Division at illinoisattorneygeneral.gov. Also file with the BBB. If the amount is under $10,000, small claims court in St. Clair or Madison County is an option. Always pay deposits by check or credit card — never cash.
