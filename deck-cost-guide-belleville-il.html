Wood vs Composite: Which Is Right for You?

Pressure-Treated Wood

  • Lower upfront cost — typically 40–50% less than composite
  • Requires staining/sealing every 2–3 years
  • Can warp, splinter, or crack over time in Metro East's freeze/thaw cycles
  • Lifespan: 15–25 years with maintenance
  • Best for: budget-conscious builds, investment properties, shorter-term ownership

Composite Decking (Trex, TimberTech, Fiberon)

  • Higher upfront cost — but almost zero maintenance
  • Fade and stain resistant — holds up to Illinois summers and winters
  • No splinters, no annual sealing required
  • Lifespan: 25–30 years with minimal upkeep
  • Best for: forever homes, families with kids, anyone tired of deck maintenance

Our honest take: If you're staying in your home 10+ years, composite usually wins on total cost of ownership. If you're selling in 3–5 years, pressure-treated wood gives you a better return on investment.

What Changes the Price

These are the factors that push a deck quote up or down the most on Metro East jobs:

Ground slope and height

A flat yard with a deck sitting 18 inches off the ground needs far fewer materials than a sloped lot where the deck is 6–8 feet up. More height = more posts, more concrete footings, and more labor. This is one of the biggest variables in Metro East where yards vary significantly by neighborhood.

Number of stairs and landings

Stairs are labor-intensive. A single 4-foot stair run adds $800–$2,000. A full wraparound stair with landing adds $3,000–$6,000+.

Built-in features

  • Built-in benches: $400–$1,200
  • Deck lighting (post caps, under-rail, step lights): $800–$3,000
  • Pergola or shade structure: $3,500–$12,000
  • Built-in planter boxes: $300–$800 each

Demo and haul-away of existing deck

If you have an old deck that needs to come down first, add $800–$2,500 for demo and haul-away depending on size and material.

Permit fees

Most Belleville-area municipalities charge $150–$600 for a deck permit. We include permit coordination in every project — you don't have to chase the city.

Permits for Decks in Belleville IL

This is one of the most searched questions we get. Here's the straightforward answer:

  • Attached deck over 200 sq ft → permit required in most Metro East municipalities
  • Deck more than 30 inches off the ground → permit required
  • Freestanding deck under 200 sq ft under 30 inches → may not require permit (varies by city)
  • Replacement of deck boards only (no structure change) → typically no permit needed

Permit requirements vary by city. Belleville, O'Fallon, Fairview Heights, Edwardsville, and Collinsville each have their own rules. We pull permits for every project that requires one — it protects you legally and ensures the work passes inspection.

Warning: Decks built without required permits can cause problems when you sell your home. Always use a contractor who handles permit coordination. All-Pro does this on every job.

How to Avoid Overpaying for a Deck

Get 2–3 written quotes

Never accept a verbal estimate. A written quote shows the exact scope — materials, size, hardware, labor — so you can compare apples to apples. If a contractor won't put it in writing, walk away.

Ask about permit handling

Some contractors quote low but hand permit responsibility back to the homeowner. That adds time, stress, and sometimes cost. Ask: "Do you pull the permit, or do I?"

Check reviews and ask for references

A $2,000 cheaper quote from an unknown contractor can cost you $10,000 in repairs if the work is done wrong. Check Google, HomeAdvisor, and Angi. Ask for the names of 2 recent customers you can call.

Be specific about your scope

The more detail you give a contractor, the more accurate the quote. Know your approximate size, material preference, and whether you want stairs or extras before you call.

Watch out for these red flags

  • Requires large cash deposit upfront (50%+ before work starts)
  • No written contract or estimate
  • No address or verifiable local history
  • Can't give you a permit reference number
  • Pressures you to sign the same day
