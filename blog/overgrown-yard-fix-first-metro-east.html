Overgrown Yard in Metro East IL? What We Would Fix First

By Bill Session · June 9, 2026 · Yard Cleanup Opinion

When a yard gets away from a homeowner, the instinct is to start buying plants, stone, or new features. Our opinion is different: clean it, open it up, fix the problem areas, then decide what belongs there long term.

Safety note: Tree removal, drainage, utility areas, and property-line work can involve safety and local-rule issues. Verify requirements and use qualified help where needed.

First: Remove What Is Hiding the Yard

Old brush, dead shrubs, volunteer trees, leaves packed against the house, and broken edging hide the real shape of the property. A proper landscape cleanup makes the next decision easier because the homeowner can finally see the yard again.

Second: Handle Trees and Drainage

Before installing new beds or mulch, look at tree limbs, deadwood, low areas, and water flow. If a limb is over the roof or water is washing through a bed, those issues should not wait until after cosmetic work. Our tree service and cleanup work often connects directly to drainage and access.

Third: Rebuild the Edges

Clean edges make a property look maintained even before major upgrades. Edging, mulch, rock, and bed shape give the yard structure. For many Metro East homes, a practical mulch or rock refresh is a better first move than a large planting plan.

Fourth: Make It Easy to Maintain

The best cleanup is the one that does not fall apart in 30 days. Leave room to mow. Avoid tiny hard-to-trim corners. Keep plants away from siding. Make sure gates and paths still work. Then set up a simple maintenance rhythm.
