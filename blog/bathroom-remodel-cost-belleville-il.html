Bathroom Remodel Cost in Belleville IL — What Homeowners Are Actually Paying in 2026

Real price ranges, what pushes costs up or down, how to phase it if budget is tight, and what to watch out for when hiring a contractor.

Quick answer: What does a bathroom remodel cost in Belleville IL?

Here are the ranges we see most often in Belleville and the surrounding Metro East area. These are local numbers — not national averages pulled from a spreadsheet:

  • Basic refresh (vanity, toilet, fixtures, paint): $4,000 – $10,000
  • Mid-range remodel (new tile, tub/shower surround, vanity, flooring): $10,000 – $25,000
  • Full master bath gut renovation (custom tile shower, double vanity, soaking tub): $25,000 – $50,000+

Most Belleville homeowners doing a standard hall or guest bathroom land in the $10,000–$18,000 range. A master bath with a custom tile shower usually runs $20,000–$35,000 depending on finishes.

What drives bathroom remodel costs up the most?

Bathrooms are expensive per square foot because of the trades involved — tile setters, plumbers, and electricians all touch the room. Here is what pushes the number up fastest:

  • Moving plumbing — relocating a toilet, sink, or shower drain requires opening the floor and rerouting pipes; add $1,500–$5,000
  • Custom tile shower — a tiled walk-in shower with a niche and bench runs $4,000–$10,000 in labor and materials alone
  • Waterproofing — any quality shower needs proper membrane waterproofing; skip this and you will pay for it in five years with mold and rot
  • Heated floors — radiant heat mats add $500–$1,500 depending on square footage; worth it in an older Metro East home
  • Expanding the bathroom — borrowing space from a closet or bedroom adds framing, drywall, and sometimes HVAC work
  • Older homes with surprises — Belleville has a lot of homes from the 1960s–1980s; once walls open, old cast iron, galvanized pipe, or knob-and-tube wiring may need attention

What keeps bathroom remodel costs down without cutting corners?

  • Keep plumbing in place — if the toilet, sink, and shower stay where they are, you save the most money right there
  • Tub surround instead of full tile — a quality acrylic surround looks clean, lasts well, and costs $800–$2,500 installed vs. $4,000–$8,000 for custom tile
  • Stock vanities from big box stores — they have improved significantly; a $400 vanity with a new faucet looks sharp and saves $800–$1,500 over custom
  • Porcelain tile over natural stone — modern porcelain mimics marble and travertine well, costs less, and is easier to maintain in a Metro East climate
  • Phase the project — do the shower and tile now, replace the vanity and flooring next year if budget is tight; bathrooms phase well

How long does a bathroom remodel take?

Timeline depends on scope. Here is what to plan for realistically:

  • Basic update (vanity swap, toilet, paint, fixtures): 3–5 days
  • Mid-range with tile work: 1–2 weeks — tile work requires cure time between steps
  • Full gut renovation with custom shower: 3–5 weeks — permits, rough plumbing, tile, and inspection add up

One thing homeowners do not always expect: tile showers need time to set and cure properly. A contractor rushing a tile job to hit a deadline is a contractor cutting corners on waterproofing. We do not do that.

Do I need a permit for a bathroom remodel in Belleville IL?

Same rule as kitchens: cosmetic work does not require a permit. Swapping a vanity, toilet, or fixtures — no permit needed. But if you are moving or adding plumbing, adding an electrical circuit (required for GFCI near water), or opening walls, a permit is required in Belleville.

We pull all permits and handle inspections. If a contractor tells you permits are not needed for work that clearly requires them, walk away — they are protecting themselves, not you.

Frequently asked questions

Is a bathroom remodel worth it in Metro East IL?

Yes — bathrooms consistently rank as one of the best ROI remodeling projects. In the Belleville and O'Fallon market, a mid-range bathroom remodel typically returns 60–70% of its cost at resale, and a sharp bathroom can be the difference between a house that sells in two weeks and one that sits. Even if you are not selling, a daily-use room that is updated makes a real difference in quality of life.

What is the most important thing to spend money on in a bathroom remodel?

Waterproofing and tile work. Everything else in a bathroom is replaceable — a vanity, a toilet, a mirror. But if a shower is not waterproofed correctly, you will be opening walls in five years to replace rotted studs. Spend the money there first. The rest can be phased.

Can I use my bathroom during a remodel?

If you only have one bathroom, this matters a lot. We plan the sequence of work to minimize downtime — rough plumbing and demo first, then tile and fixtures at the end, so the room is usable as long as possible. We discuss this with every homeowner before work starts.
