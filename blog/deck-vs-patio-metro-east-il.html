Deck vs. Patio — Which Adds More Value for Metro East IL Homeowners?

Real cost comparison, maintenance reality, resale value, and what Bill recommends after 23 years of building both in Belleville, O'Fallon, and surrounding Metro East communities.

📞 Call or Text Bill Read the Comparison

The short answer

If your yard is flat and you want low cost and zero maintenance — build a patio. If your yard has a slope, you want elevated outdoor space off the back door, or resale value is a priority — build a deck. Both are good investments in Metro East. The wrong choice is usually not deck vs. patio — it is the wrong size or the wrong materials for the budget.

Cost comparison: deck vs. patio in Belleville IL

Here are the real ranges for Metro East. National cost estimator sites are not useful here — labor and material costs in Belleville are different from Chicago or St. Louis.

Deck costs (Metro East 2026)

  • Pressure-treated wood deck, 12x16: $8,000 – $14,000
  • Pressure-treated deck with steps and railing, 16x20: $14,000 – $22,000
  • Composite deck (Trex, TimberTech), 12x16: $18,000 – $28,000
  • Composite deck with pergola, 16x20+: $28,000 – $45,000+

Patio costs (Metro East 2026)

  • Basic poured concrete patio, 12x16: $4,000 – $7,000
  • Stamped concrete patio, 12x16: $8,000 – $14,000
  • Concrete paver patio, 12x16: $10,000 – $16,000
  • Large stamped concrete with fire pit area, 20x24: $18,000 – $30,000

Bottom line: a patio is almost always less expensive than a deck of the same size. The gap narrows when you upgrade to stamped concrete or pavers and widens when comparing a basic concrete pad to a composite deck.

Maintenance: what you are actually signing up for

This is where a lot of homeowners get surprised. The upfront cost is not the whole picture.

Pressure-treated wood deck

  • Needs cleaning and staining or sealing every 2–3 years
  • Boards check, warp, and eventually need replacing — especially in Metro East freeze-thaw cycles
  • A 20-year-old pressure-treated deck often needs $3,000–$6,000 in board and ledger replacement
  • Great choice if you are willing to maintain it — lasts 20–30 years with care

Composite deck

  • Costs significantly more upfront but requires almost no maintenance
  • Clean it once or twice a year — that is it
  • 25–30 year warranties are common from quality manufacturers
  • Best choice if you want to build it once and forget it

Concrete patio

  • Virtually zero maintenance — sweep it, rinse it, done
  • Illinois freeze-thaw cycles can cause cracking over 15–20 years; control joints reduce this significantly
  • Stamped concrete can fade and needs occasional sealing every 3–5 years
  • Most durable option for the money in Metro East

Paver patio

  • Individual pavers can shift in freeze-thaw; occasional releveling needed every 5–10 years
  • Weeds can grow in joints without polymeric sand or regular treatment
  • Easy to repair — replace individual pavers instead of a full pour
  • Best-looking long-term option; adds strong curb appeal

Which adds more value at resale in Metro East?

Both add value — but how much depends on what buyers in your neighborhood expect. Here is the honest breakdown:

  • In Belleville, O'Fallon, and Shiloh neighborhoods, a clean patio or deck is expected — not having one can hurt your price
  • A composite deck typically shows better in listing photos and attracts more interest from buyers who do not want maintenance
  • A large stamped concrete patio with a fire pit area or outdoor kitchen setup can be a strong selling point in the $250k–$400k Metro East market
  • A worn, gray pressure-treated deck with loose boards actually hurts value — it becomes a negotiating chip for buyers

Rule of thumb: A well-built patio or composite deck returns 60–80% of cost at resale in Metro East. A neglected wood deck can return negative value. Condition matters more than material.

When a deck is the right choice

  • Your yard slopes away from the house — a deck spans the grade change cleanly
  • You want space directly off the back door at floor level
  • You plan to add a pergola, built-in seating, or an outdoor kitchen on a raised platform
  • Your neighborhood has mostly decks and buyers will expect one

When a patio is the right choice

  • Your yard is flat or has a slight slope — no need to build up
  • Budget is the primary concern — same square footage for less money
  • You want zero maintenance for the next 20 years
  • You want a larger entertaining space — concrete is easier to size up without the cost jump a deck would carry
