What Changes the Price the Most

Two same-size decks can vary by $10,000+ based on these factors.

Size and Shape

Square footage is the base. Odd shapes, cutouts, and wraparound designs add labor. A simple rectangle is most cost-efficient.

Stairs and Landings

Each stair section adds $800–$2,500. Multiple landings or curved stairs increase cost significantly.

Height and Site Access

Ground-level decks cost less than elevated decks. Steep slopes or complex footings add cost.

Railing Type

Basic pressure-treated vs. aluminum balusters vs. glass panels can add $2,000–$8,000+.

Lighting and Extras

Post cap lights and step lighting add $500–$2,500. Built-in benches add additional cost.

Permit and Inspection

Permit fees typically run $150–$500. All-Pro coordinates all permit requirements for every project.

Replacing an Old Deck?

Demo and Removal

Tearing out an old deck typically adds $500–$2,000. We haul everything away — no dumpster rentals.

Repair or Replace?

If the frame is structurally sound, board replacement can cost $3,000–$8,000 vs. full replacement. We will tell you honestly which makes more sense.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size deck can I get for $15,000 in Metro East?

In Metro East Illinois, $15,000 typically builds a 12×16 to 14×20 pressure-treated deck with standard railing and one stair section.

Do I need a permit for a deck in Belleville or O'Fallon IL?

Yes. Most deck projects require a building permit, especially decks over 30 inches from grade. All-Pro coordinates the permit process on every project.

How long does it take to build a deck?

Most Metro East deck projects take 3–7 days once materials arrive. Larger multi-level decks can take 7–14 days.

Is composite decking worth the extra cost in Illinois?

For most homeowners staying 10+ years — yes. Illinois freeze-thaw cycles are tough on wood. Composite handles extremes without warping or splintering. No annual sealing required.
