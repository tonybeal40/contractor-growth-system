🔥 SEP-IRA — Save $15,000–$18,000 in Taxes ACTION REQUIRED

A SEP-IRA lets Bill put money away for retirement AND reduce his tax bill dollar-for-dollar. On ~$257K net income, he can contribute up to $64,224.

What If Bill Doesn't Have the Full Amount?

He does NOT need the full $64K. ANY amount helps. Here's the savings at different levels:

How to Open a SEP-IRA (15 minutes, free)

  1. Pick a provider (all free to open): Fidelity Schwab Vanguard
  2. Click "Open a SEP-IRA" — need SSN, EIN, business name
  3. Fund the account (transfer from bank) — put in whatever Bill can afford
  4. Choose an investment (target date fund or S&P 500 index = simple)
  5. In FreeTaxUSA: go to Deductions > Self-Employment Retirement and enter the amount contributed

⚠ Deadline

Contribution MUST be made before the filing deadline (including extensions). If Bill files an extension, he has until October 15, 2026 to contribute for 2025.

What If Bill Can't Contribute Right Now?

  • File an extension (free on FreeTaxUSA) — buys time until October
  • Open the SEP-IRA account NOW (free) — fund it later before the extension deadline
  • Even a small contribution saves money — $5K saves ~$1,500 in taxes
  • If he truly can't: skip it, file without it, and set one up for 2026 instead
