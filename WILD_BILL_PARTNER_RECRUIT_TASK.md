# Wild Bill Task: Bill's List Partner Recruit List

## Goal

Build the first useful contractor recruitment base for Bill's List Metro East.

This list is for business development and partner recruiting. It is not a homeowner spam list.

## Deliverable

Create a working list of local complementary contractors that could be invited onto a future Bill's List marketing or partner page.

Target output:

- 20 to 40 companies
- grouped by category
- focused on Belleville, O'Fallon, Shiloh, Swansea, Fairview Heights, Collinsville, Edwardsville, Glen Carbon, Maryville, and Troy
- sourced from public business websites, public business profiles, chambers, associations, or other lawful public business listings

## Columns To Capture

- Company name
- Category
- City
- Public phone
- Website
- Contact URL or public business email
- Source URL
- Fit notes
- Public signal that they may need help
- All-Pro competitor: yes / no / partial
- Outreach status
- Do-not-contact status

## Best Categories First

Start with complementary and overflow-friendly trades:

1. Electrician
2. Plumber
3. HVAC
4. Roofer
5. Tree service
6. Junk removal
7. Cleaning / make-ready
8. Flooring / drywall / paint
9. Concrete specialist
10. Fence specialist

Direct competitors can be reviewed later, but the first network should not train direct competitors before All-Pro has the lead flow working.

## Fit Signals

Look for companies with public signs that they may value lead flow or website help:

- no website or only a weak Facebook page
- outdated website
- slow or broken website
- missing city/service landing pages
- no quote form
- old project photos
- weak Google Business Profile content
- strong trade skill but poor online presentation
- public hiring/sales language
- paid ads already running
- active social posts but no conversion page

## Usefulness Standard

The list is only useful if it supports:

1. a contractor-facing partner page
2. direct recruitment calls
3. category-by-category rollout
4. a clean opt-out record

Do not spend the whole run collecting names. Turn the list into an asset: a page structure, a call list, or outreach drafts.

## First Outreach Script

Phone opener:

> Hey, this is Tony with Bill's List Metro East and All-Pro. We're building a local project-match lane for Metro East homeowners, starting with Belleville and O'Fallon. I found your business while looking for reliable local pros in [category]. Are you taking new [category] jobs right now if the fit is good?

If yes:

> Good. We're not selling random shared internet leads. The idea is a local partner lane with clear project details, city, timing, and homeowner contact info. Would you be open to looking at a simple partner page and telling me if your company should be on the shortlist?

Email/contact form version:

> Subject: Metro East project referrals for [category]
>
> Hi [name/team], I found [company] while building the first Bill's List Metro East partner shortlist for local homeowner projects around [city]. We're starting with a small group of real local pros by category, not a fake directory.
>
> If you are taking new [category] jobs, I would like to confirm your service area and see whether you want to be considered for future project-match referrals.
>
> Tony / Bill's List Metro East
> 618-581-0676

## Compliance Rules

- Use public business contact details only.
- Record the source URL for every company.
- Do not scrape private/personal residential data.
- Do not use dark-web data, leaked lists, or bought consumer lists.
- Do not send automated texts or calls without proper consent.
- Do not impersonate a homeowner or a neutral directory.
- Honor do-not-contact and opt-out requests immediately.
- Keep outreach short, honest, and personalized.
