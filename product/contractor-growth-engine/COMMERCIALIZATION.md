# Commercialization Checklist

This repository is publicly visible and has no open-source license. Public visibility is not the same as granting reuse rights, but a production product should live in a private repository with client data separated by account.

Before selling this system:

1. Move the reusable product to a private repository.
2. Have an attorney review the customer agreement, privacy terms, review workflow, texting consent, data retention, and subcontractor/referral disclosures.
3. Define exactly what each plan includes and avoid ranking, lead-count, or revenue guarantees.
4. Use a signed authorization for Google, Meta, Nextdoor, Yelp, analytics, DNS, and form access.
5. Store secrets in the host's encrypted secret manager, never in Git or generated HTML.
6. Give every client an export and offboarding process for leads, analytics, content, and domains.
7. Keep a proof ledger for photos, reviews, licenses, awards, ratings, and business claims.
8. Test accessibility, mobile layout, performance, forms, and redirects before every launch.

## Suggested Product Boundary

- **Foundation:** website, form routing, analytics, technical SEO, and profile cleanup
- **Growth:** Foundation plus content calendar, review workflow, monthly reporting, and conversion improvements
- **Operator:** Growth plus CRM pipeline, approved social publishing, lead-response automation, and quarterly strategy

Price after discovery. Do not publish made-up missed-revenue numbers or imply guaranteed placement in Google, Maps, or AI answers.

This document is an operating checklist, not legal advice.
