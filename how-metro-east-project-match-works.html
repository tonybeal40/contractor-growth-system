How the local match works

How Bill's List Metro East helps homeowners start the right project path.

Instead of sending homeowners into a giant lead marketplace, Bill's List starts with a simple local intake: what you need done, where the project is, how soon you need help, and whether you want one strong contractor or help comparing options.

All-Pro handles many requests directly. With a homeowner's permission, another participating local contractor may be contacted when the project is a better fit for that provider.

The homeowner path

Clear, local, and simple. The goal is to help a homeowner take the next step without chasing random callbacks or wondering who received their request.

1

Tell us what you need

Share the service, city, project details, photos-ready status, timeline, and budget range. The more specific the request, the better the follow-up.

2

We sort the project fit

Deck repair in Belleville, a bathroom remodel in O'Fallon, and a landscape cleanup in Edwardsville all need different questions and proof.

3

All-Pro gets first look

For core All-Pro services, the request can move straight into a direct estimate path with Bill and the All-Pro team.

4

A better-fit provider may help

With your permission, project details may be shared with a participating local contractor when another provider fits the trade, city, or timing better.

5

You review the details

Ask about the written scope, pricing, schedule, insurance, applicable licensing, permits, and who will perform the work.

6

You choose whether to hire

Submitting a request does not obligate you to hire All-Pro or any participating contractor.

Questions homeowners and contractors ask

Short answers explain who may receive a request, what the service costs, and what homeowners should verify before hiring.

Is Bill's List the same as customer reviews or Thumbtack?

No. It is a local project-start page owned by All-Pro. The goal is a clearer Metro East path, not a national shared-lead marketplace.

Can a request go to another company?

Yes, but only as described in the consent statement. Project details may be shared with a participating contractor when that provider is a better fit for the request.

Can contractors apply?

Yes. Local companies can request a free listing review or ask about future paid partner options on the contractor listing page.
