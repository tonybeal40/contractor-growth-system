How Metro East Project Match Works

This system is built to do what random quote forms do badly: collect the important job details upfront, separate serious homeowners from vague tire-kickers, and create a cleaner next step than “call three contractors and hope somebody answers first.”

1. Homeowner tells us the real situation

The intake form captures the city, project type, budget range, timeline, quote intent, photos-ready status, and job details. That gives structure to the lead before anyone even makes a phone call.

2. The lead gets scored

Urgency, budget, city, service type, and homeowner intent all matter. A deck repair in Belleville with photos ready and an ASAP timeline is not the same as a “just researching” patio idea with no budget or timing.

3. All-Pro is the first contractor lane

Right now, the system is built to feed All-Pro first. That keeps the business honest: one real contractor brand, one real project pipeline, one real CRM.

4. The network grows later

Once enough demand data exists, the same intake layer can route overflow or non-fit jobs to vetted partners. That is where the local marketplace move starts to become real.

What this is A homeowner intake and demand-routing system for Metro East Illinois home-service jobs.
What this is not Not a fake city listing strategy. Not a duplicate contractor profile trick. Not a disguised scammy lead form.
Why this matters Pages can be copied. A growing local demand graph with lead quality, response speed, and outcome history is much harder to copy.
Where to start If you are ready to talk now, use the match desk or direct quote form. If you are still comparing, start with the guide layer first.
