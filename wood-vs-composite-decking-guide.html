Cost Comparison: Wood vs Composite

The biggest factor for most homeowners. Here are installed costs from real Metro East jobs:

The real cost comparison is over 20 years. Wood requires $300–$600 in stain/sealer every 2–3 years plus occasional board replacement. Composite requires almost nothing. Over 20 years, the total cost gap often narrows to $2,000–$5,000 — or composite wins outright.

Maintenance: The Real Difference

Pressure-Treated Wood

  • Needs staining or sealing every 2–3 years to prevent rot, graying, and cracking
  • New wood must dry 6–12 months before first stain application
  • Annual cleaning recommended to prevent mildew
  • Screws and fasteners may back out over time — periodic re-tightening needed
  • Boards may splinter as wood ages — especially in high-traffic areas

Composite Decking

  • Annual wash with soap and water — that's it
  • No staining, no sealing, no painting
  • Capped composite (Trex, TimberTech Azek) resists stains, fading, and mold better than uncapped
  • Virtually no splinters
  • Fasteners stay put — composite doesn't shrink/expand as much as wood

Lifespan in Metro East's Illinois Climate

This is where Illinois homeowners need to pay close attention. Metro East gets:

  • Hot, humid summers (90°F+ with high humidity)
  • Cold winters with freeze/thaw cycles
  • Spring/fall rain that keeps wood damp for extended periods
MaterialLifespan (with maintenance)Lifespan (neglected)
Pressure-Treated Wood15–25 years8–12 years
Cedar15–20 years8–12 years
Composite (standard)20–25 years20–25 years
Composite (capped premium)25–35 years25–35 years

Metro East's freeze/thaw cycles are particularly hard on wood. Water gets into the grain, freezes, and expands — causing cracking and warping over time. Composite handles freeze/thaw much better.

Appearance and Feel Underfoot

Wood

Fresh-stained wood has a warmth and character that composite can't fully replicate. It looks "natural" in a way composite still struggles to match. The trade-off: without regular maintenance, it grays and looks weathered within 2–3 years.

Composite

Modern composite has come a long way. Capped composite (Trex Transcend, TimberTech Terrain) has realistic wood grain texture and a wide range of colors. It won't fade significantly or gray out over time. The one genuine downside: dark composite gets noticeably hotter in direct summer sun — plan for lighter colors on south-facing decks.

Who Should Choose What

🪵 Choose Wood If…

  • Budget is the top priority
  • You plan to sell within 5 years
  • You want a natural, warm aesthetic
  • You don't mind annual maintenance
  • It's a rental or investment property

🏆 Choose Composite If…

  • You're staying 10+ years
  • You have kids or entertain frequently
  • You want zero annual maintenance
  • Splinters are a concern
  • You want a deck that looks good in 15 years

Our honest recommendation: If you're staying in your home more than 7–8 years, composite wins on total value almost every time in Metro East's climate. If you're selling soon or working with a tight budget, pressure-treated wood is still a solid choice — just commit to the maintenance schedule.
