Wood vs Vinyl vs Chain-Link: Which Is Right for You?

Wood Privacy Fence

  • Lower upfront cost — $22–$35/ft installed
  • Needs staining or sealing every 2–3 years to prevent rot and graying
  • Can warp or lean over time with Metro East's freeze/thaw cycles
  • Lifespan: 15–20 years with maintenance
  • Best for: budget-conscious homeowners, shorter-term ownership, rustic look

Vinyl Privacy Fence

  • Higher upfront cost — $28–$45/ft installed
  • Zero maintenance — wash with a hose once a year
  • Won't rot, warp, or need painting — holds up well in Illinois winters
  • Lifespan: 25–30+ years
  • Best for: forever homes, busy families, anyone tired of painting fences

Chain-Link Fence

  • Cheapest option — $12–$22/ft installed
  • No privacy — but great for containing pets and kids
  • Almost zero maintenance
  • Lifespan: 20+ years
  • Best for: pet containment, utility areas, rental properties

Our honest take: If you want privacy and plan to stay in your home 10+ years, vinyl pays for itself. If budget is the priority or you just need containment, wood or chain-link gets the job done.

What Changes the Price

Linear footage

The single biggest factor. Measure your yard before calling — more accurate numbers mean more accurate quotes. The number of corners and post count also affects cost.

Terrain and obstacles

Sloped yards require stepped or racked fencing — more labor. Tree roots, buried utilities, and rocky soil all add time and cost. Metro East yards vary significantly from neighborhood to neighborhood.

Gates

Each gate adds $400–$1,800 depending on type. A walk gate for a pool area costs less than a double drive gate for vehicle access. Don't forget to budget for gates when pricing your project.

Old fence removal

Removing an existing fence adds $3–$8 per linear foot for tear-down and haul-away. A 200 ft fence removal adds roughly $600–$1,600 to the project.

Post depth and concrete

Taller fences require deeper posts — a 6 ft fence needs posts set 2 ft deep; an 8 ft fence needs 3 ft. More depth = more concrete and more labor. Clay soil common in Metro East can slow digging significantly.

Fence Permits in Metro East Illinois

Permit rules vary by municipality. Here's the general picture for Metro East:

  • Fence over 6 ft tall → typically requires permit in most Metro East cities
  • Front yard fence → usually requires permit and has height restrictions (often 4 ft max)
  • Corner lots → sight-line restrictions apply — check setback rules before building
  • Backyard fence under 6 ft → may not require permit (varies by city)
  • HOA neighborhoods → HOA approval often required before permit — check your covenants

How to Avoid Overpaying for a Fence

Know your linear footage before calling

Walk your property line and measure or use Google Maps to estimate. Contractors who quote without measuring are guessing. You want a firm per-foot price on a verified measurement.

Get written quotes — not ballpark numbers

A written quote should specify: linear footage, material brand/grade, post size, gate count, and whether old fence removal is included. Compare scope, not just total price.

Ask about post quality

Some contractors cut costs by using undersized posts or skimping on concrete. 4×4 posts are minimum for 6 ft privacy fence; 4×6 is better. Ask what post size is included in your quote.

Watch for these red flags

  • No site visit before quoting — just a "ballpark over the phone"
  • Can't provide proof of liability insurance
  • Large cash deposit required (50%+) before work starts
  • No written contract or detailed scope
  • Pressure to sign same day
