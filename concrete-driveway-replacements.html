Concrete Driveway Replacements in Metro East, IL

A failing driveway isn't just an eyesore — cracked, heaved, and uneven concrete is a safety hazard and a liability. All-Pro Construction performs full driveway replacements from demolition through final pour, using proper concrete mixes, steel reinforcement, and control joints specified for Illinois freeze-thaw cycles.

What's included in a driveway replacement

  • Full demolition and removal of old concrete slab
  • Sub-base grading, compaction, and preparation
  • Reinforcement — rebar or wire mesh per design
  • Proper concrete mix for Illinois climate (typically 4,000 PSI)
  • Control joint placement to manage cracking
  • Broom finish, exposed aggregate, or smooth finish options
  • Cleanup and haul-away of all demolition debris

Signs your driveway needs replacement

  • Large cracks wider than ¼ inch
  • Multiple heaved or sunken panels
  • Crumbling edges and surface spalling
  • Drainage issues causing pooling water near the house
  • Driveway over 25–30 years old with widespread deterioration

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I repair or replace my driveway?

Minor cracks and isolated issues can often be repaired. When cracking is widespread, panels are heaved, or the driveway is simply old, replacement is the more cost-effective long-term solution. We'll give you an honest assessment at the estimate visit.

How thick should a new concrete driveway be?

Residential driveways should be a minimum of 4 inches thick, with 5–6 inches recommended for areas subject to truck or heavy vehicle traffic. We'll recommend the right thickness for your specific use case.

How long before I can use my new driveway?

We recommend waiting at least 7 days for light vehicle traffic and 28 days before parking heavy vehicles or equipment on new concrete.
